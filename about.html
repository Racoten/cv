About me


I guess I can start on how and why I came to love Cyber Security and stuff



Story time


I started loving cyber security because ever since I was a kid, I used to love computers and video games and just really love technology I first started learning C++ because my dad was the one who told me to focus in, but after that I switched to python and just fell hard for how easy the language was and how fast you could make small scripts to full programs.


After getting comfortable with python I began taking hacking courses. I liked Zaid Sabih's a lot because he explained them at a very beginner paced which was good since hacking is such a big subject. I learned almost everything from taking his other courses too. Because I was attending college I wasn't very focused on them but I was constantly thinking in my head on how the attacks worked. I also began to see how easy it was to create hacking programs in a programming language: for me it was Python obviously. And so I switched on over learning different Python libraries like: socket, beautifulSoup4, requests, paramiko and others to aid me in my exploitation practice. But because I was still in college then I wasn't going very fast, I was taking my time.


Once I felt comfortable with beginner hacking concepts and techniques, I shifted my focus on getting my first certification: the eLearn Security Junior Penetration Tester. I knew this was a big step for me because finally I could proof I could become a professional penetration tester some day. After getting that certification, I spent just about every day I could practicing in platforms like: hackthebox, tryhackme, vulnhub, root-me. Then after some time I discovered a Udemy teacher called Jesse Kurrus who had courses on vulnerable machines somewhat similar to those of OSCP. I immediately fell in love with the content and just everything he was doing. That's when I finally felt like the skills I learned I was putting them into good use.


Just this summer of 2021 I was taking my toughest exam yet: the eCPPTv2, it was hard... I mean there were times I really questioned if I was any good. But I persevered. Fun fact, before the exam, I wasn't ready for the buffer overflow part. Up to this point, I could never get that reverse callback. It was during the exam, and brutally painful to finally get the exploit working. I knew That if I couldn't crack the buffer overflow, I was *NOT* gonna pass the exam. I screamed when I got the reverse shell callback.


Now I'm just constantly doing HackTheBox and Vulnhub machines. I'm not a noob but I'm definitely not the best at hacking. I think everyone I've met who are into hacking are way better than me. I learn a lot from them... stuff I didn't even ever considered or just forgot. All in all, I like where I am right now and I will keep learning and practicing until my hands hurt... almost always but still I don't normally stop.
